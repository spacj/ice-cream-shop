# Video Sources & Recommendations

This document lists recommended sources and services for obtaining video content for the Pistacchio website.

## Quick Start Options

### Option A: Stock Video + Minor Editing (1-2 weeks, €100-500)
1. Find stock gelato/ice cream videos
2. Edit to required specs
3. Upload to website
4. **Best for**: Fast launch, budget-conscious

### Option B: Professional Videography (4-6 weeks, €2,000-5,000)
1. Hire local videographer in Utrecht
2. Shoot at your actual shop
3. Professional color grading
4. **Best for**: Premium brand, long-term use

### Option C: Hybrid Approach (2-4 weeks, €500-1,500)
1. Use stock video for hero background
2. DIY film the gelato-making process
3. Edit and color grade together
4. **Best for**: Balance quality and cost

## Video Production Services

### Local to Netherlands

#### **Dutch Production Companies with Food Specialization**
1. **Tasty Content** (Amsterdam)
   - Focus: Food videography
   - Services: Full production, color grading, sound design
   - Cost: €2,000-€5,000 per project
   - Website: Check local listings

2. **Food Photography NL** (Various cities)
   - Focus: Food product videography
   - Services: Professional equipment, editing
   - Cost: €1,500-€4,000 per project

#### **How to Find Local Videographers**
- **Directories**: 
  - https://www.productioncompanies.nl
  - https://www.filmbouw.nl (Dutch industry association)
- **Platforms**:
  - https://www.productionbase.co.uk (European coverage)
  - Local Facebook groups for Utrecht videographers
- **Freelance**:
  - Upwork.com (filter for Netherlands)
  - Fiverr.com
  - 99designs.com (video section)

#### **What to Request**
- **Deliverables**:
  - MP4 files (H.264 1920x1080, 30fps)
  - WebM backup files
  - High-resolution poster images
  - Raw footage (for future re-editing)
  - Color-graded final exports
- **Turnaround**: 3-4 weeks
- **Revisions**: Include 2-3 revision rounds
- **Rights**: Ensure you own the videos (all rights transfer)

## DIY Video Production

### Equipment (Budget-Friendly)

#### Minimum Setup (€100-300)
- Smartphone (iPhone/Android) - use existing
- Tripod (€15-30) - stabilization
- Lighting kit (€50-150) - ring light or basic softbox
- Backdrop (€20-50) - white/neutral background
- Microphone (optional, €30-100) - for audio

#### Mid-Range Setup (€500-1,500)
- GoPro or compact 4K camera (€300-500)
- Tripod + stabilizer (€100-200)
- Professional lighting kit (€100-300)
- Microphone + recorder (€100-200)
- Editing software (free or €20-50/month)

#### Professional Setup (€1,500-3,000+)
- Mirrorless camera (Canon M50, Sony ZV-E1): €600-1,000
- Lenses (kit lens + macro): €200-600
- Professional lighting: €300-800
- Audio equipment: €200-500
- Tripod/gimbal stabilization: €100-400
- Editing software (Premiere/DaVinci): €20-55/month

### Software (Editing & Color Grading)

#### Free Options
1. **DaVinci Resolve** (Recommended)
   - Professional color grading
   - Video editing
   - Multi-track audio
   - Free version is powerful
   - Learning curve: Moderate
   - **Download**: https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve/

2. **Shotcut**
   - Open-source video editor
   - Cross-platform (Windows, Mac, Linux)
   - Learning curve: Beginner-friendly
   - **Download**: https://shotcut.org

3. **HitFilm Express**
   - Video editing + visual effects
   - YouTube optimization
   - Learning curve: Moderate
   - **Download**: https://hitfilm.com/express

4. **CapCut** (Desktop)
   - Easy to learn
   - Quick edits and effects
   - Learning curve: Beginner-friendly
   - **Download**: https://www.capcut.com

#### Paid Options (Professional)
1. **Adobe Premiere Pro**
   - Industry standard
   - Integrated with other Adobe apps
   - Cost: €55/month
   - Features: Complete professional toolkit

2. **Final Cut Pro**
   - Mac-exclusive
   - Professional quality
   - Cost: €299 one-time purchase
   - Features: Optimized for Mac hardware

3. **Sony Vegas Pro**
   - Windows-only
   - Good value for money
   - Cost: €399
   - Features: Professional editing, effects

## Recommended Path Forward

### For Fastest Launch (1-2 weeks)
**Cost: €100-300**
1. Find stock gelato video on Pexels/Pixabay (hero)
2. Edit to 8-15 seconds
3. Find stock food preparation video
4. Edit to 30-60 seconds (gelato-making)
5. Compress and upload

**Stock Video Search Strings**:
- "gelato making"
- "ice cream scooping"
- "artisan dessert"
- "food preparation close up"

### For Best Quality + Brand Control (4-6 weeks)
**Cost: €2,000-5,000**
1. Hire local Utrecht videographer
2. Shoot at your actual shop
3. 2 days of filming (8-10 hours each)
4. Professional editing and color grading
5. Deliver multiple formats (MP4, WebM, 4K, 1080p)

**Videographer Briefing Should Include**:
- Shop location and availability
- Your product shots you want (pistachio highlights)
- Brand color reference (creams, greens, caramels)
- Desired tone (premium, artistic, educational)
- Sample videos you like (reference material)

### For Balance (2-3 weeks, €500-1,000)
**Cost: €500-1,000**
1. Use stock video for hero background (€0-100)
2. DIY film gelato-making (use your kitchen/shop)
3. Edit with free DaVinci Resolve (€0)
4. Color grade to brand palette (learn during process)
5. Upload and optimize

**Equipment Needed**:
- Smartphone camera (already have)
- €50-100 tripod
- €50-150 basic lighting kit
- €200-300 in extras
- 20-30 hours of your time

## Questions to Ask Videographers

1. Do you have experience with food videography?
2. What is your portfolio with similar products?
3. What is included in your package (shooting days, edits, revisions)?
4. Do you provide both MP4 and WebM formats?
5. What are your revision limits?
6. Who owns the rights to the videos?
7. Can you deliver raw footage?
8. Timeline from shoot to final delivery?
9. Payment schedule?
10. Backup plan if something goes wrong?
